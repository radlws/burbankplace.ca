About

Burbank Place Apartments Limited is a co-operative community consisting of 36 units. Owners or shareholders own shares that represent capital stock in the corporation and give them exclusive rights to their units. The community is managed by the people that live here, namely those appointed as board of directors of Burbank Place Apartments. Shareholders take turns in sitting on the board as well as helping around the community. This is the secret of keeping maintenance fees low and maximizing the enjoyment for the entire community.

Members

Burbank Place Apartments has no landlord, the members run the co-op and make all the major decisions. At the Annual General Members Meetings, for example, elections may be held for positions on the Board of Directors, or the Annual Operating Budget may be ratified. Members participate in various ways such as volunteering on to be on the board or help around the building in other ways like painting, snow shoveling etc.. Others show their commitment to the community by taking part in special projects, planting beautiful gardens, picking-up litter and participating in spring and fall clean up events and otherwise by being good neighbours.

Pets

Burbank Place Apartments is a pet friendly Community. Dogs, cats, and animals in containers or cages in numbers in accordance with municipal by-laws and the buildings Rules & Regulations.

Interested in a unit that's up for sale? Burbank Place Apartments celebrates the diversity of its shareholders. It is committed to creating a community free from discrimination under the Canadian and Ontario Charter of Rights.

Board membership criteria:

  • Commitment to co-op principles and a willingness to participate in the buildings decision-making and meetings. For example, members are expected to attend general annual meetings, serve on the Board of Directors or volunteer to take on other initiatives.
  • Demonstrated financial responsibility. (Credit Score)
  • Willing to reside in the co-op for at least one year.
  • Positive attitude to living in a community with people of diverse social, economic and cultural backgrounds. (Reference Check)
  • Maintain co-op property and own unit in good condition.
  • A person who will respect the rights of others.
  • Clear police record check.
For more details please download and examine the Rules & Regulations Handbook. Please note that all potential candidates must be interviewed and approved by the board of directors. Unfortunately, Burbank Place Apartments is currently not wheelchair accessible making it difficult to accommodate those with Accessibility Needs.

The Board of Directors

Participation on the Board of Directors is an excellent way to learn about the running a cooperative community as well as managing a property. The Board recommends by-law amendments for approval by shareholders at the Annual General Meetings. Board members approve financials, review monthly financial statements and meet yearly with Accountants to review the operating budget before it is brought to the General Membership for approval. The board meets at least once a month to oversee the financial management of the co-operative. Some of the functions include assisting in preparing the annual budget, monitoring the buildings financial position and arrears, and regularly reporting & communicating to the Board of Directors and the shareholders. If a shareholder falls into arrears, the Board meets with that member and attempts to find a solution, while at the same time balancing the buildings need to operate as a financially viable organization. Board members must be fair and impartial and willing to devote one or two evenings each month. The board of directors duties can be broken into finance, member selection, building improvements and maintenance, social and living issues, as well as regular operating activities. Elections are held each year after the close of the fiscal year.

The Maintenance Fees

Please note that maintenance fees and divided up according to a formula (see Rules & Regulations Handbook: Maintenance Calculations). The fees are subject to change on a yearly basis based on the established budget for that year. The fees usually range from the low 200's for the bachelor units to the mid 300s for the two bedroom units. Unit hydro and unit insurance are not included in the maintenance fees. All shareholders must have unit insurance. For more information on maintenance fees speak to the owner of the unit you are interested in or your real estate agent.

The Location

Burbank Place Apartments is just minutes from Hamilton's downtown core, is located on the north edge of the Niagara Escarpment. Check out the location on the map on the right hand side! The building is located close to bus stops on Stinson, King and Main making most amenities only moments away. For more information on public transportation, the Hamilton Street Rail (HSR) website has transit routes and schedules. There are many surrounding parts including Bishops Park and Carter park which includes a children's playground and a basketball court. A variety store is a few steps away on Stinson St. and a plaza including a grocery store, a pharmacy and other stores is within walking distance. On Victoria street you will find a gas station and a Canadian tire. Central Memorial Recreational Center which includes a swimming pool, a gym amongst other amenities is just a few blocks away.

Close to nature

Close to the Wentworth Stairs as well as access to Hamiltons Rail Trail, the building is within easy walking distance to nature and fitness activities. The Bruce Trail (Rail Trail) on Wentworth Street South is reachable via the Wentworth Street Stairway. The trail cuts through the city along the Niagara Escarpment (mountain) and used by many locals for a full days hike. The Trail is a total of 430 miles long and starts at Niagara Falls! It passes through Hamilton and ends at the Bruce Peninsula.
